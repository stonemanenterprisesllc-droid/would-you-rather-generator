How to play Would You Rather

The rules are as simple as a game gets: one person reads a "would you rather" question with two options, and everyone else has to pick one — no skipping, no "both," no "neither." The fun is in defending your choice and hearing why everyone else went the other way. It's perfect for parties, classrooms, road trips, sleepovers, team icebreakers, and dinner-table conversation.

Ways to use it

  • Icebreaker: go around the group, each person answers and gives one reason why.
  • This-or-that vote: everyone points left or right at the same time — count the split.
  • Hot seat: one person answers ten in a row as fast as they can.
  • Kids & classrooms: switch to the "For kids" category for clean, silly fun.

Categories in this generator

Choose Funny for absurd silliness, Hard choices for genuine dilemmas that spark debate, Gross for the ick-factor questions kids love, For kids for wholesome options, or Deep for questions that reveal what people actually value. Pick Random to draw from every category at once.

Tips for better questions

The best "would you rather" questions have two options that are genuinely close — if one choice is obviously better, there's nothing to argue about. Generate a batch, keep the ones that split your group down the middle, and follow every answer with the real question: "why?" Everything is generated in your browser; nothing is stored.
